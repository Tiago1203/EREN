# ADR-0052: Department Hierarchy Model

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-16

**Deciders:** Architecture Board

---

## Context

Hospitals have departments (ICU, Emergency, Radiology) that cut across the physical hierarchy. A patient in Room 302 (floor 3, building A) can be in the ICU department.

---

## Decision

**Departments are a separate organizational hierarchy from the physical hierarchy.**

A patient/bed belongs to both:
1. A **physical location** (Campus → Building → Floor → Room → Bed)
2. A **department** (Department hierarchy)

---

## Department Hierarchy

```
Organization
└── Department Group (e.g., Clinical Services)
    └── Department (e.g., ICU, Emergency, Radiology)
        └── Unit (e.g., ICU-North, ICU-South)
```

---

## Patient Assignment

A patient is assigned to:
- A physical bed (via Room → Floor → Building → Campus)
- A department (via Department hierarchy)

Both assignments are independent and can change during hospitalization.
