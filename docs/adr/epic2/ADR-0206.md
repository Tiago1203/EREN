# ADR-0206: Patient and Diagnosis Scope — Not EPIC 2

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-16

**Deciders:** Architecture Board

---

## Context

`apps/api/app/domain/` currently contains `patient/` and `diagnosis/` directories. EPIC 2 is supposed to cover Device, Incident, Recommendation, and Knowledge. Patient and Diagnosis belong to the Clinical Domain, which is handled in EPIC 5 (Clinical Intelligence), not EPIC 2.

---

## Decision

**Patient and Diagnosis are NOT part of EPIC 2. They belong to the Clinical Domain (EPIC 5).**

### Patient Domain (Clinical Domain, EPIC 5)
- Aggregate Root: `Patient`
- Bounded Context: Patient Context
- Owner: Clinical Domain
- Location: `core/patient/` (future EPIC 5)

### Diagnosis Domain (Clinical Domain, EPIC 5)
- Aggregate Root: `Diagnosis`
- Bounded Context: Diagnosis Context
- Owner: Clinical Domain
- Location: `core/diagnosis/` (future EPIC 5)

**Current state:** `apps/api/app/domain/patient/` and `apps/api/app/domain/diagnosis/` exist as stubs. They are not fully implemented and are NOT part of EPIC 2.

**Action required:**
1. Move `apps/api/app/domain/patient/` to `apps/api/app/clinical/patient/` (temporary location for EPIC 5)
2. Move `apps/api/app/domain/diagnosis/` to `apps/api/app/clinical/diagnosis/` (temporary location for EPIC 5)
3. Do NOT create `core/patient/` or `core/diagnosis/` until EPIC 5
4. Remove from `apps/api/app/domain/` to avoid confusion

**Why temporary location in `apps/api/app/clinical/`:**
- Clinical domain entities will live in `core/` when EPIC 5 is implemented
- `apps/api/app/clinical/` is a staging area — marks these as pending EPIC 5
- Do not create `core/clinical/` yet — wait for EPIC 5 design

---

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Clear scope for EPIC 2
- No confusion about what belongs where
- Clean `apps/api/app/domain/` for EPIC 2 orchestration only

**Negative:**
- Refactoring needed to move files
- Clinical domain stubs are incomplete — they will be completed in EPIC 5
